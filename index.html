Electrical Safety Data Sheet

This OSHA standard highlights the importance of keeping electrical equipment in safe working condition, especially in construction environments

  • Preventing explosions caused by electrical faults
  • Keeping equipment free from dust that may cause malfunctions
  • Regular inspections and repairs to avoid safety risks
  • Reporting damaged circuits immediately and stopping use until they’re fixed
  • Electric shock
  • Burns
  • Fire hazards
  • Jewellery risks
  • Tripping over loose wires or poorly placed cables
  • Muscle injuries from involuntary shock reactions
  • Rubber gloves and safety footwear
  • Face shields or goggles
  • Flame-resistant clothing
  • Insulated sleeves
  • Respirators (when needed)
  • Always use Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures
  • Use GFCIs on all power tools
  • Keep all tools grounded and insulated
  • Don’t work in damp or wet conditions
  • Clearly label all equipment and circuits
  • Use a standard 16-section SDS format
  • Label all hazardous materials properly
  • Maintain a written safety communication plan
  • Train staff in a language they understand
  • Ensure SDS documents are easy to access
